creXRtive — Human–Computer Interaction Lab

Branding & Web Development

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, HTML, CSS

February 2023 - May 2025

1. Overview

creXRtive is a Human–Computer Interaction lab at Sabancı University focused on extended reality technologies, immersive interaction techniques, and user-centered digital experimentation. My role included developing the lab's visual identity and designing a web presence to represent ongoing research projects, team activities, and long-term academic goals.

The branding work was created alongside a collaborative VR project developed for the lab, leading to a visual identity aligned with technical research environments rather than commercial branding styles.

2. Background & Design Direction

The identity needed to serve academic, research-oriented, and publication-level communication rather than commercial promotion. Because the lab operates under an institutional umbrella, the visual system was designed to feel consistent with university-level branding while still reflecting the lab's focus on XR technologies.

The design direction emphasizes:

  • Clarity over decoration
  • Minimal geometry and structured layouts
  • Professional and research-driven presentation
  • Visual consistency across posters, documentation, and web assets

Instead of expressive branding, the goal was to establish a credible, functional, and long-lasting identity suitable for scientific contexts.

3. Early Logo Explorations

Before finalizing the identity, multiple logo concepts were developed to explore different interpretations of spatial interaction and digital immersion. Early drafts experimented with geometric forms, 3D-inspired shapes, XR symbolism, and more expressive visual styles.

These initial concepts helped define limitations: overly decorative or experimental forms did not align with the academic tone needed for the lab's institutional role. Through these iterations, the brand direction shifted toward a more restrained and formal structure.

4. Final Logo Design

The final logo adopts a clean, minimal, and structured typographic approach, prioritizing clarity and institutional compatibility. Rather than encoding thematic metaphors or illustrative XR references, the design uses subtle geometric balance and linear precision to suggest technical rigor in a refined way.

This version aligns more closely with Sabancı University's visual identity, ensuring that the lab can appear professional in grant proposals, conference materials, academic presentations, and official publications. The restrained design also enhances longevity by avoiding trendy or stylized aesthetics.

5. Typography System

The typographic system follows a neutral and academic style, using a single sans-serif type family across platforms. The aim was not to create expressive typographic contrast but to support legibility in research contexts such as posters, reports, and VR project documentation.

Hierarchy is created through scale and weight variations rather than decorative fonts. This approach ensures consistency between internal lab materials and broader institutional branding standards while maintaining a clean and technical appearance.

6. Website Design

Following the finalized identity, I designed a website to present the lab's vision, research focus areas, team members, and ongoing XR projects. The structure uses a modular, grid-based layout to organize complex information in a way that is accessible to students, collaborators, and external audiences.

The interface emphasizes:

  • Minimal surface visuals
  • Strong alignment and spacing
  • Neutral typography
  • Simple navigation and clear hierarchy

The site serves as both a communication platform and a portfolio of research outcomes.
